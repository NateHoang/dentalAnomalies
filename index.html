Enamel anomalies

Anomaly Definition / Key features
Fluorosis Mottled enamel from excess fluoride; white chalky patches to yellow/brown mottling.
Febrile-related enamel defects Enamel malformation (bands/pitting) caused by high childhood fevers affecting ameloblasts.
Tetracycline-induced staining Intrinsic dark banding from tetracycline binding to developing tooth calcium.
Amelogenesis imperfecta (AI) Inherited enamel formation defects (hypoplastic, hypomaturation, hypocalcified) → thin/soft/discolored enamel.
Enamel hypoplasia Pits, grooves or large areas of deficient enamel, often from childhood illness or antineoplastic therapy (Turner’s tooth example).
Deepened pits & grooves (developmental) Incomplete enamel maturation → weak, caries-prone areas from ameloblast disruption.
Enamel pearl Ectopic enamel on root bifurcations/trifurcations that may impair periodontal attachment.
Cervical enamel extension Flat elongated enamel from crown toward furcation, linked to furcation problems and periodontal attachment loss.

Dentin anomalies

Anomaly Definition / Key features
Dentinogenesis imperfecta (DGI) Hereditary dentin defect → opalescent/blue-brown teeth, bulbous crowns, obliterated or abnormal pulp chambers; Shields types I–III.
Dentin dysplasia Type I (radicular) "Rootless" teeth with short/absent roots, periapical radiolucencies; crowns usually normal.
Dentin dysplasia Type II (coronal) Primary teeth resemble DGI; permanent teeth have thistle-tube pulp chambers and pulp stones.
Regional odontodysplasia (affects dentin + enamel + pulp) Localized developmental abnormality → “ghost teeth” (thin enamel/dentin, large pulp), often fails to erupt.

Tooth shape / structural anomalies

Anomaly Definition / Key features
Gemination Single enlarged tooth from attempted division; usually one root canal, normal tooth count.
Fusion Two tooth buds fused into one large tooth; typically shows missing tooth in count and separate root canals.
Concrescence Teeth joined at roots by cementum, often following inflammation.
Dilaceration Abnormal angulation/bend of root or crown, commonly post-traumatic.
Dens invaginatus (dens in dente) Invagination of enamel/dentin into tooth; Oehler’s Types I–III (crown → through-root).
Dilated odontoma Severe invagination causing a dilated, malformed tooth (complication of dens invaginatus).
Dens evaginatus Accessory cusp/tubercle (often premolars) containing enamel/dentin/pulp → high risk of pulp exposure.
Talon cusp Large accessory cusp on lingual of anterior teeth (≥½ CEJ→incisal); most common on maxillary laterals.
Taurodontism Enlarged pulp chamber with apically displaced floor and shortened roots; associated with syndromes.
Supernumerary roots Accessory roots (radix entomolaris/paramolaris)—important for endo/extraction planning.

Size / number / eruption anomalies

Anomaly Definition / Key features
Diffuse microdontia Generalized small teeth (seen in Down syndrome, pituitary dwarfism).
Isolated microdontia (pegged laterals) Single small/conical tooth (e.g., pegged lateral incisor).
Macrodontia Teeth larger than normal, associated with hemifacial hyperplasia/gigantism.
Disturbances in tooth number Anodontia, Hypodontia, Oligodontia, Hyperdontia (supernumerary), Impaction.
Ankylosis (localized eruption disturbance) Tooth fused to alveolar bone with absent PDL → halted eruption/infraocclusion.

Resorption & pulpal alterations

Anomaly Definition / Key features
Internal resorption Pulpal injury → resorption from inside, balloon-shaped radiolucency; “pink tooth” if coronal.
External resorption Resorption of external tooth surfaces from trauma, surgery, orthodontics, pressure from lesions.

Tooth wear

Anomaly Definition / Key features
Attrition Tooth-to-tooth wear (incisal/occlusal surfaces).
Abrasion Mechanical wear by external agents (toothbrush, abrasive materials).
Abfraction Cervical loss from tooth flexure / occlusal stress.
Erosion Chemical dissolution of tooth structure (acidic foods, vomiting).

Discoloration (extrinsic & intrinsic)

Anomaly Definition / Key features
Extrinsic discoloration Surface stains: bacteria, tobacco, foods/beverages, restorative materials — often removable.
Tetracycline staining Intrinsic dark banding from tetracycline chelation with calcium during development.
Hyperbilirubinemia-related discoloration Greenish tooth color from bilirubin deposition in developing teeth.
Porphyria (Gunther disease) Red-brown intrinsic staining; primary teeth both enamel & dentin affected, permanent only dentin.
Fluorosis (also an enamel anomaly) White/brown mottling from excess fluoride.

Other (cementum / root)

Anomaly Definition / Key features
Hypercementosis Excessive cementum deposition at root(s); radiopaque thickened roots, associated with local/systemic factors.
